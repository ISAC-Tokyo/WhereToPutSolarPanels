Our planet, Earth, is a source of various but limited energy resources and home of a fast-growing population that increasingly requires more energy.

This situation thus led science, technology and policies to focus on searching for better energy sources for quite long time already. Moreover, global disasters---such as the tsunami in 2011 that damaged a nuclear power plant in Fukushima, Japan---led to re-evaluate the viability of current energy production means, and to consider safer alternative sources.

Solar energy is one type of renewable energy that is gaining popularity in many countries in recent years.

The industry is now able to build more efficient solar panels that produce electric energy in cost-effective ways. One problem, though, is that the energy input of solar panels depends on weather conditions, notably the rate of sunlight exposure. It should be noted that energy output of same solar panel technology may differ based on its location, thus yields different return on investments as well.

Where to put solar panels?

The aim of this application is to find the best spots where to install the solar panels. How is it possible? The idea here is simple: Finding areas with fewer cloud cover! This system determines which areas have less cloud by analyzing historical satellite data from 2001-2012 and by evaluating expected solar power generation. Spots with the best sun exposure in the past can then be used to model return on investment and to guide decisions on where to put-up the panels.

Install a solar panel at your current location...

You can simulate the sun exposure where you are and estimate the electricity you can generate to recover the installation cost.

You can evaluate a location from scientific data collected by the MODIS Earth

Heat-map and time series represent the data visually so as to ease the search for cloudless spots.
